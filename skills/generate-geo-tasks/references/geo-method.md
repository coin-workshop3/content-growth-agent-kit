# GEO task method

## Four layers

1. AI-answer discovery: phrase a question a real buyer may ask and define the entities and evidence needed for a useful answer.
2. Platform search: combine product, scenario, region or platform, and intent language without keyword stuffing.
3. AI parsing: state which product name, scenario, proof, boundary, and next action must be unambiguous in the content.
4. Monitoring: preserve a stable prompt ID and later record mentions, recommendation position, citations, factual accuracy, and competitor share.

## Task quality gate

A task is ready only when:

- the query resembles customer language;
- intent is one of learn, compare, buy, avoid-risk, replace, or local-search;
- the scenario is concrete enough to guide content;
- entities are explicit;
- required proof is available or marked missing;
- the answer can state a boundary instead of making an unlimited promise.

Prioritize tasks that combine commercial intent with evidence the company can actually show. A high-volume phrase without proof or a realistic decision context is not automatically high priority.
